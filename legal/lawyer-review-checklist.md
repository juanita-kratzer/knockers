# Lawyer review checklist — Knockers platform

**Purpose:** Structured checklist for qualified legal counsel. Do not rely on this document as legal advice.

**Documents to review:**  
`privacy-policy-draft.md`, `terms-and-conditions-draft.md`, `entertainer-contractor-agreement-draft.md`, `user-booking-agreement-draft.md`

---

## 1. Contractor classification

- [ ] Confirm that the classification of entertainers as **independent contractors** (not employees) is valid under applicable Australian law (e.g. Fair Work, ATO guidelines).
- [ ] Consider multi-factor tests (control, expectation of work, tools, risk, etc.) and whether platform design supports contractor status.
- [ ] Advise on any changes to product or wording to reduce risk of recharacterisation as employment.

---

## 2. Platform liability exposure

- [ ] Review **limitation of liability** and **exclusion** clauses in Terms & Conditions for enforceability under Australian Consumer Law (ACL) and state law.
- [ ] Consider whether disclaimers for "quality" of entertainer services are sufficient and clearly communicated.
- [ ] Advise on any gaps (e.g. negligence, safety, data breach) and recommended caps or carve-outs.

---

## 3. Group liability clause legality

- [ ] Review the **"client responsible for guests"** / group liability concept in User Booking Agreement and Entertainer Contractor Agreement.
- [ ] Confirm enforceability of making the booking user liable for guest behaviour (contractual indemnity / responsibility).
- [ ] Consider tort vs contract and any statutory limits; advise on wording and prominence.

---

## 4. Chat data access legality

- [ ] Confirm that **collecting, storing, and accessing in-app chat messages** for service delivery, disputes, and safety is compliant with privacy law (e.g. Privacy Act 1988, APP).
- [ ] Advise on retention periods, disclosure to parties in a dispute, and disclosure to law enforcement or safety bodies.
- [ ] Consider whether consent and Privacy Policy wording are sufficient.

---

## 5. Age verification sufficiency

- [ ] Advise whether **18+ age requirement** plus self-declaration is sufficient, or if positive age verification (e.g. ID) is required before certain actions (e.g. booking, messaging).
- [ ] Consider regulatory expectations (e.g. state laws, advertising) and risk of underage use.

---

## 6. Police check integration legality

- [ ] Review use of **police checks** (e.g. "hard" profile) for compliance with spent convictions, discrimination, and privacy law.
- [ ] Advise on what can be requested, stored, and displayed (e.g. "verified" badge) without breaching legislation.

---

## 7. Cancellation enforcement

- [ ] Confirm that **cancellation fees and penalties** (client and entertainer) are enforceable under contract and consumer law.
- [ ] Consider unfair contract terms (UCT) and whether terms are transparent and fairly presented.
- [ ] Advise on chargebacks and payment provider (Stripe) requirements.

---

## 8. Stripe Connect compliance

- [ ] Advise on **Stripe Connect** (or equivalent) usage: platform as facilitator, entertainers as connected accounts, fee splitting, and any required disclosures or agreements.
- [ ] Confirm that payment and payout terms in Terms and Contractor Agreement align with Stripe’s requirements and financial services regulation.

---

## 9. Safety button liability implications

- [ ] Consider **liability exposure** from the in-app "Safety" / emergency alert feature (e.g. if user relies on it and we fail to act, or we do act and third parties are involved).
- [ ] Advise on disclaimers, terms of use of the feature, and any recommended limitations or notices.

---

## 10. Insurance recommendations

- [ ] Recommend **insurance types** (e.g. public liability, professional indemnity, cyber, D&O) for the platform operator.
- [ ] Advise whether entertainers should be required to hold their own insurance and how to reflect that in the Contractor Agreement.

---

## 11. Content moderation obligations

- [ ] Advise on **legal obligations** (if any) to moderate user-generated content (profiles, chat, reviews) under Australian law (e.g. defamation, harmful content, eSafety).
- [ ] Consider reporting and takedown procedures and any safe harbour or defence considerations.

---

## 12. Australian Consumer Law considerations

- [ ] Review all consumer-facing terms for **ACL** compliance: unfair contract terms, consumer guarantees, misleading or deceptive conduct, and mandatory disclosures.
- [ ] Ensure guarantees and refund rights are not excluded beyond what the ACL allows.
- [ ] Advise on "business" vs "consumer" users if both use the platform and whether different terms are needed.
